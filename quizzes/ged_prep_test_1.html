GED Math Practice FAQ

What topics are covered in the GED Math Test?
The GED Math test covers four main areas: Basic Math/Number Operations (25-30%), Algebraic Concepts (30%), Geometry (20%), and Data Analysis & Probability (15-20%).
Is this practice test similar to the real GED exam?
Yes, these are advanced multiple-choice questions based on official GED test guidelines and format. The questions are designed to match the difficulty level of the actual exam.
How long is the actual GED Math test?
The official GED Math test is 115 minutes long and includes approximately 45 questions, including multiple-choice, drag-and-drop, hot spot, and fill-in-the-blank formats.
What score do I need to pass the GED Math test?
You need a score of at least 145 out of 200 to pass the GED Math test. A score of 165-174 indicates college readiness, and 175+ demonstrates college readiness with potential credit.
Can I use a calculator on the GED Math test?
Yes, you can use the on-screen calculator for most of the GED Math test. There is a small section at the beginning where calculators are not allowed.
